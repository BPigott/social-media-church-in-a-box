# Bible Study Guide Generation

## Purpose
Create small group Bible studies that move people from **information to encounter** through discussion, reflection, and practical application.

## Structure: The Small Group Bible Study

1.  **Title & Big Idea:** One sentence summarizing the sermon's main takeaway.

2.  **Getting Started (Icebreaker):** A warm, non-threatening relational question.

3.  **Read the Text:** The main passage(s) from the sermon.

4.  **Discussion Questions:**
    * **Observation ("Head"):** 2-3 questions to understand the text.
        - "What words or phrases stand out?"
        - "What did this mean to its original audience?"
    * **Interpretation ("Heart"):** 2-3 questions connecting the text to the sermon's theme.
        - "How does this passage reveal what God is *really* like?"
        - "What habit, story, or assumption in our own lives does this challenge?"
    * **Application ("Hands"):** 2-3 questions focused on *action*.
        - "How is the Holy Spirit prompting you to respond to this?"
        - "What would it look like to *practice* this truth in your family, workplace, or neighborhood this week?"

5.  **The Weekly Practice:** A clear, tangible spiritual practice for the group to commit to doing before they next meet (e.g., practicing Sabbath, fasting from media, etc.).

6.  **"Do Something, Tell Someone":** A final challenge that includes **both** an action and accountability.
    * *Example:* "Your challenge is to **(Do Something)**: Find one practical way to serve someone in secret this week. Then, **(Tell Someone)**: Be ready to share with the group next week how it felt and what you learned."

7.  **Ministry Time (Prayer):** Conclude with an instruction to pray *for one another*, inviting the Holy Spirit to empower, heal, speak, or give gifts.
    * *Example:* "Get into groups of three. Share one area where you need the Holy Spirit's power to live this out. Then, pray for each other, asking the Spirit to come and equip you."

## Question Guidelines

* **Be Open-Ended:** Never a "Yes/No" question. Start with "What," "How," and "Where."
* **Be Practical, Not Abstract:**
    * *Bad:* "How can we conceptualize God's love?"
    * *Good:* "What is one specific way you can *show* God's love to a coworker this week?"
* **Be Personal & Introspective:** Ask questions about habits, thoughts, and "the stories we believe."
* **Be Spirit-Expectant:** Assume the Holy Spirit will speak and act.
* **Be Holistic (Up, In, Out):** Ensure questions cover all three dimensions:
    * **Up (With God):** "How does this change the way you pray or worship?"
    * **In (With Self):** "What personal habit or mindset is this truth inviting you to change?"
    * **Out (With Others):** "Who in your life needs to hear this good news? How can you be an agent of justice or mercy in this area?"

## Tone Requirements

* **Warm & Sincere:** Humble, compassionate, and authentic
* **Relational, Not "Religious":** Use contractions and conversational language
* **Accessible (No Jargon):** Avoid "Christianese" - explain theological terms
* **Practical & Grounded:** Always provide concrete, actionable steps
* **Hopeful & Empowering:** Focus on God's empowerment
