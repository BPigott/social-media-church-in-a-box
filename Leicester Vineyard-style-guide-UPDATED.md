# Leicester Vineyard Communications Style Guide

## Church Overview

**Leicester Vineyard** is a church in the Vineyard movement, based in Knighton, Leicester, meeting every Sunday at 10:30am at Sir Jonathan North Community College, Knighton Lane East, Leicester, LE2 6FU. Refreshments are available from 10:15am. LV Kids groups open from 10:20am.

**Website:** www.leicestervineyard.org

**Social Media Handles:**
- Instagram: @LeicesterVineyardChurch
- Facebook: facebook.com/VineyardLeicester

**Vision Tagline:** WAY * TRUTH * LIFE (drawn from John 14:6 — "I am the way and the truth and the life")

**Vision Statement:** Leicester Vineyard is a Bible-based church community that seeks to follow the way, understand the truth of who God has made us to be, and live a fruitful life led by the Spirit.

---

## 1. This Season's Ministry Focus

Leicester Vineyard's current ministry emphasis has two movements that should shape all content in this season:

**Invitation** — The church is outwardly focused. Leicester is our home and mission field. We exist to welcome people in, to introduce them to Jesus, and to make the church genuinely accessible to anyone curious about faith. Every piece of communication is, at some level, an invitation.

**Discipleship** — Once people are in, we want to help them go deeper with Jesus. This is not just attendance; it's transformation. Following Jesus is a journey, and the church exists to walk that journey with people through community, Scripture, prayer, and the Holy Spirit's work.

These two movements — Invitation and Discipleship — are the lens through which content should be created. Ask: is this drawing people in, or helping them go deeper? Ideally, both at once.

---

## 2. The Five Values

These five values underpin everything Leicester Vineyard communicates. Reference them when generating content.

1. **We are powered by prayer.** We act in expectation of God's Kingdom breaking into our lives through the Holy Spirit. We take every opportunity to listen, discern, and step out in faith.

2. **We are outward looking.** Leicester is our home and mission field. We show and tell others about Jesus, staying generous and invitational to those around us.

3. **We are servant hearted.** We follow Jesus by serving those in need in our church, community, and city. We put others before ourselves and respond with passion and generosity.

4. **We are committed to doing things well.** Whatever we do in ministry, we want it to reflect the heart of Jesus: meaningful, considered, and sustainable.

5. **We are family.** We are an informal and relational community where everyone is known, loved, and finds belonging. We welcome the new, the least, and embrace difference.

## 3. Voice and Tone

Leicester Vineyard communicates with warmth, clarity, and conviction. The church takes its faith seriously without being formal or exclusive.

### Core Principles

- **Be direct and clear.** Say what you mean. Avoid padding or corporate language.
- **Be inclusive.** Use "we" and "us". Avoid language that implies insiders and outsiders.
- **Be hopeful.** Words should carry the energy of a community that expects God to move.
- **Be real.** Authenticity matters more than polish. Speak from lived experience.
- **Be kind.** Even hard messages can be delivered with grace.
- Write as you would speak to a friend. Plain English, no jargon.

### Primary Voice Characteristics

- **Warm and Inviting:** Communication should feel genuinely welcoming, never exclusive or judgmental.
- **Authentic and Relational:** Prioritise real stories, personal connections, and genuine community experiences.
- **Hopeful and Empowering:** Emphasise transformation, God's presence, and the possibility of changed lives.
- **Accessible and Clear:** Avoid overly complex theological language; explain concepts in everyday terms.
- **Inclusive and Compassionate:** Reflect the church's values of compassion and generosity toward all people.
- **Humble and Servant-Minded:** Focus on serving the community and supporting others' journeys, not promoting the church.

### Tone Variations by Context

**Sundays and Worship Events:** Celebratory, joyful, and encouraging. Emphasise community gathering and God's presence.

**Pastoral Communication:** Tender, supportive, and present. Acknowledge struggles while pointing toward hope.

**Small Groups:** Intimate, relational, and conversational. Feel like peer-to-peer communication.

**Outreach and Invitation:** Warm, curious, and non-pressuring. Genuinely interested in people exploring faith.

**Leadership Updates:** Transparent, thoughtful, and honest. Acknowledge seasons and changes with clarity.

**Teaching and Biblical Content:** Thoughtful, exploratory, and practical. Help people apply Scripture to real life.

## 5. Language Preferences

### Preferred Language

- "We believe..." / "Our heart is..." (ownership and clarity)
- "Join us" / "You're welcome" (inclusive invitation)
- "WAY * TRUTH * LIFE" (anchor tagline — use in headers and recurring touchpoints)
- "Invitation" and "Discipleship" (the two key movements of this season)
- "Going deeper with Jesus" (discipleship language)
- "Life transformation" / "Lives changed" (concrete outcomes)
- "Community" / "Church family" (relational framing)
- "Explore faith" / "Journey with Jesus" (non-pressuring language)
- "Encouraged and supported" (pastoral language)
- "God's Kingdom" / "God's presence" (central themes)
- "Together" / "One another" (emphasising community)

### Language to Avoid

- "You should..." / "You must..." (prescriptive, judgmental tone)
- "Non-believers" / "Sinners" (divisive categorisation)
- "Our church is the best..." (self-promotion)
- Overly formal or academic theological terms without explanation
- Guilt-based or shame-inducing rhetoric
- Exclusive language ("our kind of people")
- Hollow clichés without substance
- Jargon-heavy Christianese without context

## 6. Target Audience

### Primary Audiences

**Seekers and Explorers:** People curious about faith, exploring Christianity, or returning to church after time away. Invitation content is primarily aimed here.

**Young Families:** Parents seeking community, children's ministry, and a values-based environment.

**Community Members:** Neighbours and locals in Knighton and the wider Leicester area interested in what Leicester Vineyard offers beyond Sunday services.

**Existing Members:** Those already engaged, seeking deeper community and discipleship. Discipleship content is primarily aimed here.

**Small Group Participants:** People in mid-week community groups seeking connection and spiritual growth.

### Audience Characteristics

- Diverse age ranges and backgrounds
- Urban and suburban Leicester residents
- Mix of religious and non-religious backgrounds
- Values-driven (seeking meaning, community, and purpose)
- Relationally motivated (want authentic connections)
- Practical in faith (want to see how Jesus' teachings apply to daily life)

## 7. Scripture Usage

### Guidelines

- **Use Scripture purposefully.** Connect biblical passages to real-life application and contemporary situations.
- **Explain context.** When quoting Scripture, briefly contextualise why it matters for today.
- **Avoid proof-texting.** Don't use isolated verses to support predetermined conclusions; show the fuller biblical narrative.
- **Make it accessible.** Use clear, modern translations; explain unfamiliar concepts or cultural references.
- **Connect to transformation.** Show how Scripture leads to changed hearts and lives, not just intellectual understanding.
- **Reflect Vineyard tradition.** Emphasise Jesus' teachings, the Holy Spirit's empowerment, and kingdom values.

### Preferred Approach

- Weave Scripture naturally into teaching and communication.
- Use stories and parables from Jesus to illustrate kingdom principles.
- Highlight passages about compassion, generosity, community, and transformation.
- Connect Old Testament wisdom to contemporary life.
- Emphasise the Spirit's role in interpreting and applying Scripture.

### Scripture Style

Scripture references are set in italics with the reference noted alongside. The WAY * TRUTH * LIFE tagline should always trace back to John 14:6 as its anchor.

- **Extend God's Kingdom** throughout Leicester through visible acts of compassion and generosity.

## 9. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Invitation and Welcome
Content that invites people to explore faith, visit Sunday services, join small groups, or engage with church events. Emphasise "Everyone is welcome" and remove barriers to entry. This is the outward-facing pillar.

### Pillar 2: Discipleship and Spiritual Growth
Teaching, Bible studies, devotionals, and resources helping people go deeper with Jesus. This is the inward-growing pillar. Show practical ways to follow Jesus more closely.

### Pillar 3: Community and Belonging
Stories and content celebrating relationships, small groups, and the church family. Show diverse people connecting authentically.

### Pillar 4: Transformation Stories
Testimonies of lives changed through encountering Jesus and engaging with the church community.

### Pillar 5: Kingdom Impact and Generosity
Content highlighting the church's compassion initiatives, community service, and generosity in action.

### Pillar 6: Prayer and the Holy Spirit
Content celebrating prayer, expectation, and the experience of God's presence. Emphasise that the church expects God to move.

### Pillar 7: Practical Faith
Content showing how Jesus' teachings apply to real life: relationships, work, finances, and purpose.

### Pillar 8: Worship
Content celebrating worship and the experience of God's presence in community.

## 12. Do's and Don'ts

### Do's

- **Do lean into Invitation and Discipleship.** These are the two defining movements of this season. Every piece of content should connect to one or both.
- **Do be authentic.** Share real stories, real struggles, and real faith journeys.
- **Do invite generously.** Make every communication an open invitation; remove barriers.
- **Do celebrate community.** Highlight relationships, connections, and people stories.
- **Do explain why.** Help people understand the purpose and heart behind initiatives.
- **Do use conversational language.** Write as you would speak to a friend.
- **Do show, don't just tell.** Use stories and examples rather than abstract concepts.
- **Do anchor to the vision.** Reference WAY * TRUTH * LIFE and the five values where appropriate.
- **Do welcome questions.** Create space for exploration and doubt in faith conversations.
- **Do celebrate diversity.** Highlight the church's inclusive, welcoming nature.
- **Do focus on transformation.** Show how faith leads to changed lives and renewed purpose.
- **Do use real photography.** Include photos of actual Leicester Vineyard people, community moments, and worship experiences. Never use stock imagery.

### Don'ts

- **Don't be exclusive.** Avoid language that makes people feel like outsiders.
- **Don't oversell.** Let the authentic community story speak for itself.
- **Don't use guilt.** Avoid shame-based or fear-based messaging.
- **Don't be vague about faith.** Be clear about Jesus and the Christian message, but keep it accessible.
- **Don't use overly formal language.** Avoid corporate-speak or stiff, institutional tone.
- **Don't make assumptions.** Don't assume people know church terminology or biblical references.
- **Don't focus only on Sundays.** Highlight the full church life: small groups, prayer, and service.
- **Don't be preachy.** Avoid talking at people; invite them into conversation.
- **Don't ignore the city.** Remember the church exists to bless Leicester and extend God's Kingdom.
- **Don't shy away from Scripture.** Use the Bible confidently, but make it accessible.
- **Don't forget the Spirit.** Emphasise God's active presence, not just human effort.

## Implementation Notes

This style guide reflects Leicester Vineyard's core identity: a welcoming, relational, Spirit-empowered community committed to following the WAY, knowing the TRUTH, and living a fruitful LIFE led by the Spirit.

In this season, the two movements of Invitation and Discipleship should be felt in all communications. We are a church that has its doors wide open to Leicester, and that walks with everyone who comes through those doors toward a deeper life with Jesus.

All communications, whether Sunday announcements, social media posts, small group invitations, devotionals, or leadership updates, should embody warmth, authenticity, and a transformational focus.

**Key Principle:** Every communication is an invitation. Whether explicitly stated or implicitly felt, people should sense they are genuinely welcome and that their presence matters.
